# FTIR Instrument Profile

FTIR measures wavelength-dependent infrared absorption behavior and is commonly used for functional-group screening and fingerprint comparison.

## What the signal can support

- Approximate positions of dominant absorption bands
- Presence or absence of broad hydroxyl or water-like bands
- Presence or absence of a strong carbonyl-like region
- Qualitative fingerprint-region comparison
- Conservative functional-group screening

## What the signal cannot support alone

- Unique compound identity
- Definitive phase identity
- Exact composition or purity
- Reaction mechanism or conversion
- Absolute absorbance or transmittance claims when the response channel is unlabeled
